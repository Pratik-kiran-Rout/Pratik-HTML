Volcanic Mountains

Formed by volcanic activity, these mountains feature craters and are often characterized by the type of lava and eruptive history. Examples include Mount St. Helens and volcanic ranges like the Hawaiian Islands.

Fold Mountains

The most common type, formed when two or more tectonic plates collide, causing rocks to be deformed and folded. Examples include the Himalayas, the Rocky Mountains, and the Appalachian Mountains.
